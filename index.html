Sustainability

A village economy that powers itself

Every stay sits inside a working circular economy — waste becomes energy, and energy comes back to the community.

Farm Waste & CattleStep 1
Biogas ProductionStep 2
Methane Power PlantStep 3
Electricity GenerationStep 4
Community UsageStep 5
01

Cattle and crop waste from every homestead is collected daily by the village cooperative.

02

Biogas digesters convert that waste into methane, cutting firewood use in kitchens.

03

A shared power plant burns the methane to drive small generators built for village-scale load.

04

Electricity is metered and distributed fairly across homes, homestays and workshops.

05

Leftover slurry returns to the fields as fertiliser, closing the loop back to step one.

Our Commitment

Built on four promises

100% Community Participation

Every homestay, guide and craft session is run by a village family, not an outside operator.

Renewable Energy Usage

Biogas, solar and small hydro cover the electricity needs of partner villages.

Cultural Preservation

Tourism income directly funds Chhau troupes, weaving cooperatives and folk music schools.

Sustainable Tourism Practices

Visitor numbers are capped per village so growth never outpaces the land or the culture.
